CHAIRMAN'S MESSAGE

Among all bodily organs, one knows that eyes are very important, as was said in Sanskrit sloka Sarvendriyanam Nainam Pradhanam for a man the life becomes miserable if he lacks eye vision and even world becomes total dark. Everything has to be found only by grooping though sun rises and day breaks for his life there is not sun rise both day and night are a kin to him. Even if it is a path of roses but for him it’s a path full of thorms. Due to his blindness was unable to get any employment and hence to be starved.

Inspite of this, another reason for the miserable life, the people are ignorant and unaware of their maintenance of eyes and the cleanliness. As to avoid all the above said conditions Asaya Youth Association was founded and conducting eye camps in many village areas. Till now in Mandals like Cheepurupalli, Garividi, Merakamudidham etc., Around 3000 members got benefited by getting chance of free surgery for eyes. Our mission is to bring hight to the blind and give new life to them and lead their own life. Due to illiteracy, ignorance, no awareness for good health and many of such, I lost my mother while I am in fourth year. Then onwards my life is moving along with associated problems. As my motto is to see that no one should suffer like me and this idea made me to move towards this directions creating health awareness in people leading them to avail the facilities provided by the government for them etc are my ideas.

When shared my ideas with my friends they also appreciated and accepted my views and were ready to accompany me. Thus our pioneer step lead us to establish a trust of this kind in 2006 was named Asaya. From then onwards without any support and dependence on any other organization we are running this trust. We are extremely happy for receiving appraisals from people in around these areas.

-R.RAMANA (Chairman)

Success of Asaya Trust

Success of Asaya Trust

Asaya Youth Association has achieved many things by serving humanity.

Asaya Trust has served many villages by supplying medical help to rural poor. It was really a boon to the villagers. Nearly 400 villages were benefited by Chicken Guniya and Dengue Pills.

Free Eye Camps were conducted for nearly 5000 people, who were suffering from severe Eye diseases.

“Donate Blood and Save Life” slogan was raised to bring awareness among the people. Nearly 2000 people participated in this camp and donated blood to 2000 patients.

In the same way programme on saving Environment, ban of Plastic use, planting saplings. Many slogans were raised to bring awareness of saving environment and about 20,000 saplings were planted.

“Educate for all and Eradication of illiteracy” with a missionary zeal to bring awareness of education among people, A library was established.

I Reddy Ramana, Chairman of Asaya Youth Association with utmost commitment dedication my life and time to serve humanity. In 2003, when Asaya Trust was started there was no NGO in Cheepurupalli. After seeing the work of Asaya Trust many people got inspired and numbers of NGO’s were established.

DUTIES OF EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE

The committee should call for meeting at least for six times in a year. Written information should be sent to the member of the committee at least before a period of a fort-night. In an emergency period a notice can be sent to the members for immediate assembly and it should be done (for sending notice) at least with in the time of 24 hrs. 2/3[two third of members] are enough got quorum.

NORMS

  • Every one (should work regularly) irrespective of caste creed, gender, religion for the fulfillment of the trust's aims.
  • The eligibility age for becoming a member of the trust is 18 years plus The main criteria that all the measures should possess is determination, faith and hope, love at the work, dedication and perseverance.
  • Directors have right to take disciplinary actions against any member(s) based on this resolutions taken by the majority of member.
  • It is the same for recruitment. The members in any committee/wing should shoulder to take up responsible position (like awareness campaign on various issues).
  • Both the members who are involved directly or indirectly/ wholly or partially do not have any right to ask trust properties.
  • Funds are released from the trust or the outer organization that support trust in order to meet the maintenance work.
  • when trust is not in the position to release funds, the members concerned can take aid from the public directly/indirectly in terms of cooperation, material help etc.
